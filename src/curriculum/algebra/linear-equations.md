## Tutor Notes
Start with balance language instead of procedural memorization. Keep returning to the idea that every move on one side must be mirrored on the other side.

## In-Session Examples
Solve equations with one step, two steps, and distribution. Include one equation with variables on both sides and one that simplifies to no solution or infinitely many solutions.

## Common Mistakes
Students often lose negative signs, distribute incorrectly, or combine unlike terms too early. They also rush past the final substitution check.

## Practice Problems
Solve: 3x + 7 = 22. Solve: 5(x - 2) = 3x + 10. Solve: 4x + 9 = 4x + 1.

## Homework
Complete ten mixed linear equations and write one sentence explaining each major step for the three hardest problems.

## Answer Key
x = 5. x = 10. No solution.
