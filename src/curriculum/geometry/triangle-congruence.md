## Tutor Notes
Push the student to name exactly which information is known and why it is enough. The goal is disciplined proof setup, not quick pattern matching.

## In-Session Examples
Compare two diagrams and decide whether the given information supports congruence. Walk through a proof that uses SAS and a separate proof that uses HL.

## Common Mistakes
Students regularly confuse SSA with a valid theorem and forget to justify shared sides or vertical angles.

## Practice Problems
Identify the congruence theorem for three triangle pairs and explain why one additional diagram does not prove congruence.

## Homework
Write two short congruence proofs and annotate each statement with the exact theorem or postulate used.

## Answer Key
Answers depend on the diagram, but the student should consistently name the theorem and justify each shared or derived fact.
