## Tutor Notes
Keep the student focused on setup. Most stoichiometry errors come from starting with the wrong conversion chain rather than arithmetic.

## In-Session Examples
Convert grams to moles, use the mole ratio, and convert back to grams. Include one limiting-reagent preview problem if the student is ready.

## Common Mistakes
Students skip balancing, invert mole ratios, and confuse coefficient meaning with subscripts.

## Practice Problems
Solve three one-reaction mass-to-mass conversions and explain the role of the balanced equation in each setup.

## Homework
Finish a mixed stoichiometry packet with explicit unit labels written at every step.

## Answer Key
Each solution should show the balanced equation, the factor-label chain, and the final unit.
