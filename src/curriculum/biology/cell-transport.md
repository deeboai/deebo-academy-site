## Tutor Notes
Use diagrams constantly. Students usually understand the words before they can interpret what the membrane process looks like in a real cell.

## In-Session Examples
Compare diffusion, osmosis, facilitated diffusion, active transport, endocytosis, and exocytosis with one biological example for each.

## Common Mistakes
Students often say osmosis is just water moving without specifying the membrane context, and they mix up concentration gradient direction in active transport.

## Practice Problems
Classify five transport scenarios and explain whether ATP is required.

## Homework
Create a two-column comparison chart for passive and active transport with one example per row.

## Answer Key
The answer key should include process name, direction relative to concentration gradient, and whether ATP is used.
