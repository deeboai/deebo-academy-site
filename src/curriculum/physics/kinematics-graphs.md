## Tutor Notes
Require verbal interpretation at every step. If a student cannot describe the motion in words, they usually do not understand the graph yet.

## In-Session Examples
Read position-time and velocity-time graphs, compare constant and changing velocity, and interpret acceleration from graph behavior.

## Common Mistakes
Students confuse a graph's visual height with speed and forget that slope and area represent different physical ideas depending on the graph.

## Practice Problems
Explain the motion shown on four graphs and compute one slope and one signed area.

## Homework
Complete a graph-interpretation sheet and write a sentence for each interval describing the motion.

## Answer Key
Correct responses identify whether the object is moving forward, backward, speeding up, slowing down, or remaining at rest.
